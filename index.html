Nobuo Uematsu

The one genius music composer who brought many gamers and listeners with his emotional soundtracks

Nobou Uematsu in the middle left (dressed in white) while the musicians are behind him.

The time of Nobou Uematsu's background and music life:

  • 1959 - Born in Kochi, Kochi-Prefecture, Japan
  • 1970 - Starts playing the piano and writes music during his junior high school years with a band
  • 1981 - Graduates from Kanagawa University with an English degree
  • 1985 - meets Hironobu Sakaguchi, a video game director, and joins Square, a game company, to compose video game music
  • 1987 - composes music for a video game called Final Fantasy for the NES(Nintendo Entertainment System) console.
  • 1989 - composes video game music for the SaGa series (Final Fantasy Legend) of games.
  • 1990 - composes music for Final Fantasy IV and Final Fantasy V for the SNES (Super Nintendo Entertainment System) console.
  • 1992 - starts composing music for Final Fantasy VI
  • 1994 - composes music for Final Fantasy VII and joins in composing Chrono Trigger's soundtrack after Yasunori Mitsuda, another composer, got an ulcer.
  • 2000 - produces the main-theme song for Ah! My Goddess: The Movie
  • 2001 - finishes composing Final Fantasy X's soundtrack with Masashi Hamauzu and Junya Nakano
  • 2003 - assists Hitoshi Sakimoto, a composer, to compose the main theme song of Final Fantasy Tactics Advance and forms a music band called The Black Mages to produce music ablums with Kenichiro Fukui and Tsuyoshi Sekito
  • 2004 - leaves Square (known as Square-Enix currently) to form his music studio called Smile Please
  • 2005 - makes music for Final Fantasy VII: Advent Children, a CGI (Computer- Generated Imagery) film, with The Black Mages and composes Blue Dragon's and Lost Odyssey's soundtrack with Mistwalker, a game studio (Sakaguchi is the head of the company).
  • 2008 - finishes composing the main theme song of Super Smash Bros. Brawl
  • 2011 - finishes composing The Last Story's soundtrack (by Mistwalker) for the Nintendo Wii console


"For this title, I got Uematsu-san to write about forty pieces of music, and I feel that The Last Story itself changes as you listen to the music. That’s the power of Uematsu-san’s melodies. It’s not simply that the music is beautiful, it’s also the fact that his humanity comes across in them. They are instantly recognisable as his songs."

-Hironobu Sakaguchi
